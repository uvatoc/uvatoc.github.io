Some relevant links for the class:

  • Companies by Market Capitalization (Facebook is now down to $1.060, from $1.069 on Wednesday morning, so there is still hope they will fall off the list.)

  • Here’s the one paper from a Google co-founder that I could find which actuall contains a legitimate theorem and proof: Sergey Brin, Rajeev Motwani, Craig Silverstein. Beyond Market Baskets: Generalizing Association Rules to Correlations. In SIGMOD 1997. The more famous paper that includes the PageRank definition is: Lawrence Page, Sergey Brin, Rajeev Motwani, Terry Winograd. The PageRank Citation Ranking: Bringing Order to the Web. Stanford Report, 1999.

  • Bill Gates’ very legitimate theoretical computer science paper is: William Gates and Christos Papadimitriou. Bounds for Sorting by Prefix Reversal, Discrete Mathematics, 1979. This co-author also wrote my second favorite comic book on theoretical computer science: Logicomix: An Epic Search for Truth. (We won’t get to my favorite comic book until you have a deeper understanding of universal computation, but it does have a surprising indirect connection to Gill Bates’ paper.
